Ramiro Larrouyet

Waitomic

As I enter the earth’s wumb
Darkness is turned on
I willingly float into it
Where Plato’s allegory is not welcomed
Nor necessary

Absorbed by an undercover sky
The world’s chaos holds its breath
And a synodic show worms into my eyes

I am the cave astronaut
In search of earthly constellations
I am the underground astrophile
Surrounded by new galaxies

My tongue blushes
As awe gobbles all sense and words
No need for them
Nature is a language, can’t you read?

Water carries all confusion away
No fuzz nor buzz
Just the river running deep
Right through my thought

Sleeping with my eyes open,
Suspended in liquid reverie
All becomes eunoia
While I doze away in starry daydream

Blue Mountain Arts Poetry Contest, 2021.

Child’s Play

Sweat! Sweat! Sweat! Hard work won’t be paid,
Bike! Bike! Bike! Better run and hide,
Pay! Pay! Pay! Nothing will return,
Rape! Rape! Rape! Women are unheard,
Kill! Kill! Kill! Justice will forgive,
Steal! Steal! Steal! Media will conceal,
Clap! Clap! Clap! Argentina has collapsed.

The National Poetry Society, 2017.
